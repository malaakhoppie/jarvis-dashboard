## LIQUIDITY — THE BASE OF ALL DECISIONS

### Definition
Any obvious area where retail stop losses lie. Banks hunt these to fill their orders.

### 4 Types of MAJOR Liquidity
1. **Unmanipulated Doji** — within previous wick, non-FU high/low. No ATT FU manipulation.
2. **Perfect Double Top/Bottom** — exact match (not 0.1 pip off). Use broker, not TradingView.
3. **Perfect Trendline** — minimum 3 perfect rejections to classify as major.
4. **Imbalanced Candle (IMB)** — moves that start by instantly moving in one direction.

### Major vs Minor Liquidity
- **Major**: Obvious area, less manipulation on other TFs, powerful HTF backing
- **Minor**: Not as obvious, signs of manipulation present on other TFs (e.g., 30min doji = start of 4hr FU)

### The Last Area of Liquidity (LAL)
- Most concentrated and refined area of liquidity
- Where you expect the specific reaction / reversal after it's met
- Your target depends on your trading style: scalp = 1min–30min, intraday = 1hr–daily, swing = daily+
- Between LAL areas = rapid move with no reason to stop (major gap in liquidity between them)

### Calculating Liquidity (Daily Process)
1. Flick through timeframes weekly → 1hr to identify major liquidity in BOTH directions. No bias.
2. Look lower TFs (30min–1min) to determine direction: whichever side has more LTF liquidity = direction price is heading.
3. Don't fight obvious manipulation. If at sell POI but LTF shows heavy buy manipulation = wait.

### Liquidity Rules
- TP should be BEFORE the doji/liquidity is taken in full (price may react without full take)
- A doji can be met PARTIALLY only when major liquidity of opposite overpowers
- Major liquidity + overpowered by HTF manipulation = will hold for a while (next major LAL)
- Strong FU/HCS AFTER major liquidity = that liquidity holds until LAL of opposite side is met
- Only 4hr+ confirmed FU closures indicate prevalent direction (not lower TFs)
- Every session will have at least one major target within the 4 liquidity types on the 1–5min

## FU CANDLE

### Definition
A candle that takes liquidity AND makes a move in the other direction — ALL in the same candle.
Strong close (little or no rejection) = best retest opportunity.

### What It Represents
Banks in the move. Manipulating price, taking liquidity. True direction confirmed.

### FU Retest
- Safest way to trade: wait for retest of the FU wick
- Any FU retested = strong sign of direction
- Most swing highs/lows START with FU retests
- 30min FU + 15min retest = same strength. Vice versa.
- A 30min FU to downside needs 30min+ or very high 15min manipulation to negate.
- ATT FU can also be retested — use LTF confirmation

### NOT every FU is a position
Must combine: major liquidity context + TF hierarchy + X3 confirmation + overall manipulation flow.

## ATTEMPTED FU (ATT FU)

### Definition
A candle that was manipulated but didn't close as a strong FU — typically closes as a doji.

### What It Means
- Price started as FU but banked closed it as doji = minor liquidity generated
- Sign banks are stepping in but not the strongest base
- NOT a major doji target — minor liquidity only

### Two Scenarios
1. **Completed**: ATT FU taken in full (more common — ~likely)
2. **Wick retested**: FU wick retested without breaking full ATT FU (determined by LTF reaction)

### Key Use
- Distinguishing major doji vs ATT FU is critical for liquidity calculation accuracy
- ATT FU = low liquidity move (on opposite side = sign of true direction)

## FU NEGATION

### Definition
When banks form an FU in one direction, and the VERY NEXT candle forms an FU in the opposite direction = "negation."

### What It Means
- Potential change in orderflow (targeting opposite liquidity now)
- Banks' power is "exhausted" — only so much manipulation possible
- Second instance of manipulation after the first FU

### X3 Negation
- Negation of a negation = x3 manipulation
- Even stronger move being prepared
- Negation can be counted 2 candles after FU (if first candle is ATT FU)

### Use Cases
- On 1min: potential entries
- On 4hr: strong direction signals
- FU negation wick can also be retested as POI

## HIGH CONFLUENCE SETUP (HCS)

### Definition
When an FU is retested AND the retest itself forms an FU = HCS.
Most powerful single candlestick formation. Strong sign of institutional orderflow.

### Two Uses
1. **Bias/Liquidity Calc**: HCS on HTF = banks comfortable, targeting opposite side
2. **Entry Model**: LTF HCS = wait for 2nd FU to form on retest — no guesswork

### HCS Forms
- Two strong FUs retesting each other (strongest)
- ATT FU + FU negation wick (weaker form, still counts)
- Any two retesting each other

### Power Rule
- HCS = double the strength of an FU on same timeframe
- 30min HCS = equivalent of 1hr FU
- No 10min+ HCS/Negation backing = NO TRADE

## TIMEFRAME STRENGTH (TFS)

### Core Rule
Higher TF manipulation overpowers lower TF.
- 4hr FU to downside = needs 4hr+ FU to upside to negate
- Random 1min FU retest WON'T reverse 4hr move — only aligns with 4hr upside manipulation
- 4hr manipulation: only 3hr+ confirmed FU closures indicate prevalent direction

### TFS Hierarchy for Entry
- Scalp minimum: 10min+ HCS/Negation potential
- Intraday: 1hr+ HCS/Negation alignment
- Swing: 3hr+ confirmed FU closures

### Key Rule
- Lower TFs only: concerned with HCS + negation (not confirmed FU closures)
- 3hr+: watch for confirmed FU closures as direction indicator

### Session Timing (from Task 3.3)
- 11hr/Daily TF: main swing targets (300+ pip avg move when 1hr/3hr/4hr/5hr/7hr/11hr all backed)
- Base for every main entry: 15min manipulated high/low minimum
- Odd timeframes (11hr, 7hr, 5hr, 3hr) used for swing — Daily can substitute if no access

## ZONES

### Types
1. **Ordinary OB + FU Zone**: First small reaction on same TF tells you when relevant (marks start of same TF move)
2. **Zone of Manipulation**: Manipulated LTF reaction at HTF zone area (no same-TF reaction yet) — use for anticipating entries or confirming direction

### Rules
- Zones are SECONDARY to liquidity and story of manipulation — don't fixate on them
- Marking zones: most concentrated areas of manipulation. FU serves same purpose as OB.
- Best zones: Weekly + Daily + 4hr overlap
- OB + FU < HCS Zone in strength
- Liquidity and story of manipulation FIRST. Then confirm with zones.

## IMBALANCES

- Another form of liquidity — don't over-complicate
- Main type: imbalanced candle (starts moving instantly in one direction — no wick rejection)
- Use your BROKER to view imbalances (not TradingView)
- Focus on: 5min+ imbalances as targets
- True moves WON'T leave behind major imbalances
- Lower TFs will not fill every imbalance — don't chase

## THE TRUE STOP LOSS

- The most concentrated area of manipulation where buys/sells occur without breaking
- Our version of "Break of Structure" (BOS)
- When true stop is broken = banks targeting that side, liquidity past that point is next target
- Use on higher timeframes primarily
- Secondary to bias — an extra confirmation tool
